2026-06-02 17:19:20 From Hyungsuk Tak to Everyone:
	Statistical modeling often starts with the assumption that the data are randomly generated (or sampled) from a population, meaning that we might obtain a different sample if we repeated the sampling procedure. The potential variability arising from obtaining a random sample from the population of interest is described by the sampling distribution of the quantity of interest. Then, what is the random mechanism that might have generated the current data set? A measurement error model (assumption) based on parametric distributions, such as the Gaussian distribution, as emphasized in the inference class, is an effort to describe that unknown random mechanism and thereby propagate the sampling variability in the data to the uncertainty in a quantity of interest.
